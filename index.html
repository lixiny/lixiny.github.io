Research

My research interests include Computer & Robot Vision, Visual SLAM, and Robotics. Currently, I am focusing on modeling and analysis the hand-object interaction.

HybrIK: A Hybrid Analytical-Neural Inverse Kinematics Solution for 3D Human Pose and Shape Estimation
Jiefeng Li, Chao Xu, Zhicun Chen, Siyuan Bian, Lixin Yang, Cewu Lu
CVPR, 2021
arxiv / code

We address the above issues by bridging the gap between body mesh estimation and 3D keypoint estimation. We propose a novel hybrid inverse kinematics solution (HybrIK). HybrIK directly transforms accurate 3D joints to relative body-part rotations for 3D body mesh reconstruction, via the twist-and-swing decomposition. The swing rotation is analytically solved with 3D joints, and the twist rotation is derived from the visual cues through the neural network.

BiHand: Recovering Hand Mesh with Multi-stage Bisected Hourglass Networks
Lixin Yang, Jiasen Li, Wenqiang Xu, Yiqun Diao, Cewu Lu
BMVC, 2020
arxiv / code

We introduce an end-to-end learnable model, BiHand, to recover hand mesh from RGB image. BiHand adopts a novel bisecting design which allows the networks to encapsulate two closely related information (e.g. 2D keypoints and silhouette) in a single pass.
